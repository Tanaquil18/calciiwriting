An especially astute reader might notice that we said that the denominator can contain terms of the form \(ax^2+bx+c\) but we only considered terms of the form \(x^2+k\) in our examples and activities. The reason is that every \(ax^2+bx+c\) can be written as \(a\left( \left(x-\frac{b}{2a}\right)^2+k\right)\) if we recall how to complete the square from algebra class, and then using a substitution  3  turns this into some constant number multiplied by \(y^2+k\text{.}\)
